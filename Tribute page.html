S. N. Goenka

Pioneer of Secular Meditation Movement

S.N. Goenka, was a Burmese-Indian teacher of VipassanƒÅ meditation.

    Biography: life and background
  • 1924 - Born in Burma
  • 1969 - Goenka was authorised to teach by U Ba Khin, who died in 1971. He left his business to his family and moved to India, where he started teaching Vipassana meditation.
  • 1976 - He opened his first meditation centre, Dhamma Giri, in Igatpuri near Nashik, Maharashtra.
  • 1982 - He started training assistant teachers.
  • 2000 - Goenka laid the foundation of the 325 ft. high Global Vipassana Pagoda, near Gorai Beach, in Mumbai, which opened in 2009, and has relics of Buddha and a meditation hall
  • 2000 - Goenka was an invited speaker at the Millennium World Peace Summit of Religious and Spiritual Leaders at the General Assembly Hall of the United Nations in New York City.
  • 2013 - He died at his home in Mumbai.

Today, Vipassana courses, in the tradition of Sayagyi U Ba Khin, are held at 310 locations in 94 countries, of which about 176 are permanent Vipassana meditation centres. There are such centres in the Argentina, Australia, Austria, Belgium, Brazil, Cambodia, Canada, France, Germany, Hong Kong, Indonesia, Iran, Israel, Italy, Japan, Malaysia, Mexico, Mongolia, Myanmar, Nepal, New Zealand, Philippines, Poland, Russia, South Africa, South Korea, Spain, Sri Lanka, Singapore, Sweden, Switzerland, Taiwan, Thailand, The United Kingdom, The United States and 78 centres in India.
